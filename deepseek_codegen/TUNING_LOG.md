# DeepSeek-V3.2 codegen_py decode tuning log

Drives `tune-emitpy-perf` against `deepseek_codegen/graph_0/main.py` (parity-restored ŌĆö see `CODEGEN_ISSUES.md`). Target: lower the device-time of one decode step (`decode_1_start` ŌåÆ `decode_1_end` signposts in `main.py:13380`) while holding PCC vs `golden_logits.pt` (see "Harness" section below).

## Harness (PCC correctness signal)

`pcc.py` runs `_main` once and produces two signals:

* **Bootstrap PCC (soft).** Compares each of the 10 returned tensors against `baseline_outputs.pt`. PCC=1.0 means the computation is bit-identical to the saved baseline; useful for catching unintended drift in a refactor, but does NOT validate against the reference model.

* **Golden PCC (hard fail, floor=0.9).** `_main` was patched to additionally return the pre-argmax logits tensor (`ttnn_to_layout_267`) as `out[9]`. The TTNN stack has shape `(32 chips, 32 batch_pad, 129280 vocab)`; chips group as `[0..7] / [8..15] / [16..23] / [24..31]` (4 unique batch shards, replicated 8├Ś by the cluster-axis-1 all_gather), and within each chip the 32 batch rows are tile-padding for a single sequence. `golden_logits.pt` is `(128, 1, 129280)` FP32 where all 128 rows are identical (one batch's logits replicated). The canonical comparison is therefore `chip[0].row[0]` vs `golden[0]`, giving **PCC = 0.921842** on the unmodified baseline (bf16 quantization through the LM head and reduce_scatter). Hard floor is 0.9 ("as good as baseline").

The earlier convention of comparing against a self-bootstrapped baseline with threshold Ōēź 0.9 was misleading: PCC = 1.0 vs bootstrap is necessary but not sufficient evidence that an optimization is numerically safe, and a real fidelity-improving patch (e.g. HiFi4 ŌåÆ HiFi2) can legitimately read PCC < 1.0 vs bootstrap while still being Ōēź 0.9 vs golden.

E30 invalidates the "MoE matmul is unreachable" stance of the previous exit notes. ### Why the canonical "big" fused MoE ops were not used

The "Tier 1" candidates the earlier exit-note pointed at (`ttnn.experimental.moe_compute`, `selective_reduce_combine`, `deepseek_moe_reduce_scatter`) were investigated and **all three are topology-incompatible with our 4├Ś8 BH galaxy + 8-experts/device + cluster_axis=0 setup**:

- `ttnn.experimental.moe_compute` (`experimental/ccl/moe_compute/`): the nightly test (`tests/nightly/tg/ccl/moe/test_moe_compute_6U.py`) only runs on `1x8` or `1x16` single-galaxy meshes (gated by `TT_MESH_GRAPH_DESC_PATH` to one of two specific torus graph descriptors), and the DeepSeek variant `test_moe_compute_deepseek` is parameterised at `mesh_shape=(1,16)` with `experts_per_device=2` ŌĆö i.e. **32 total experts on 16 devices**, not 256 experts on 32 devices. The `validate_matmul` code path is hardcoded around an "experts in double buffer" assumption that breaks for `experts_per_device > 2`. The model-level `test_optimized_moe_decode_block.py` covers the full 256 experts but at `mesh_shape=(16,8)` = 128 devices with 2 experts/device ŌĆö also not our box. Probing `prepare_w0_w1_tensor_for_moe_compute` confirmed the packer's shard maps assume the test topologies.
- `ttnn.experimental.deepseek_moe_reduce_scatter` (`experimental/ccl/deepseek_moe_reduce_scatter/`): its test is decorated `@skip_for_blackhole("Requires wormhole_b0 to run")` ŌĆö the kernel only registers a WH program factory.
- `ttnn.experimental.selective_reduce_combine` (`experimental/ccl/moe/selective_reduce_combine/`): same `single_galaxy_1x{8,16}_torus_graph_descriptor` gating as moe_compute.

The fused MoE family is **wormhole-TG-tuned** with hardcoded `experts_per_device Ōłł {1, 2}`. Trying to wire any of them into our 4├Ś8 BH + 8-experts/device setup would not just fail validation ŌĆö the kernel itself would either reject the mesh descriptor or take a slow fallback that's strictly worse than the existing `sparse_matmul`. **Park all three until tt-metal lands a BH 4├Ś8 program factory or supports `experts_per_device=8`.**

### What worked instead ŌĆö and the lesson

The unblocker was abandoning the "one giant tt-metal kernel that swallows the whole MoE block" path and instead asking *which existing pieces of our graph share an input and therefore admit a manual structural fusion*. The two same-input matmuls (`sparse_matmul_0` gate and `sparse_matmul_1` up, both consuming `ttnn_reshape_146` + `ttnn_to_layout_262`) were the obvious target ŌĆö `sparse_matmul(input, [W_gate; W_up])` + 2 slices is the classic SwiGLU gate-up fusion that every modern LLM kernel does, and we can land it with one new `main_const_eval_*` plus a 50-line patch in `_main`, no semaphores, no sub-devices, no kernel writes.

The non-obvious tuning lesson the experiment surfaced:

> **`tt-perf-report`'s `SLOW`/`2 GB/s` hint refers to *per-core* bandwidth, not aggregate.** A sparse_matmul that "looks DRAM-bound" by that hint is often actually **core-bound** when it uses far fewer cores than the grid allows. Our `b={128} ├Ś 32 ├Ś 7168 ├Ś 2048` matmul ran at `per_core_N=6` on a `(11,10)` grid, occupying only 11 of 110 cores; doubling N to 4096 and **keeping** `per_core_N=6` brings 21 cores online instead of 11, and the new `b={128} ├Ś 32 ├Ś 7168 ├Ś 4096` matmul ran in **107,961 ╬╝s** ŌĆö *less* than the sum of the two `b={128} ├Ś 32 ├Ś 7168 ├Ś 2048` matmuls it replaced (89k + 89k = 178k ╬╝s at 11 cores each). The first attempt with `per_core_N=12` (the "obviously matched" doubling) regressed +4.8k ╬╝s precisely because per_core_N=12 still uses 11 cores. **Always check core utilisation before trusting the bandwidth-bound diagnosis.**

- **The `tt-perf-report` "output subblock Ōēź 2" hint is a generic matmul hint that does not apply to `ttnn.sparse_matmul`** (E36/E37 ŌĆö two consecutive hangs).

### E2 attempt ŌĆö what went wrong (briefly)

1. The pre op output is a tile-wide tensor (`[..., 1, 32]`) where only column 0 holds the real `╬Żx┬▓` and columns 1ŌĆō31 are undefined. ## Exit notes (after MLA-QKV survey) ŌĆö Tier-2 fused-op lane also confirmed infeasible

Re-attacked the queue's Tier-2 "MLA Q/K/V refactor via `ttnn.experimental.create_qkv_heads_from_separate_tensors` / `ttnn.experimental.nlp_create_qkv_heads_decode`" candidate after the post-E37 stop. Result: **lane is infeasible on this graph**, branch tip stays at E34 / ŌłÆ74.8 %.

### Why the fused QKV-heads ops don't fit DeepSeek MLA as emitted

`ttnn.experimental.create_qkv_heads_from_separate_tensors` requires two inputs: Q + a **concatenated K|V** packed on the last dim (`kv_input_shape[3] = 2*num_kv_heads*head_dim`), both **TILE + BLOCK_SHARDED**, with `q_input_shape[0] == num_h_cores` (batch must equal the grid height). Our MLA produces K and V on **separate** chains (K comes from `matmul_6` on the Q-up-projected latent; V comes from `slice_68(matmul_0) ŌåÆ all_gather ŌåÆ sum ŌåÆ slice ŌåÆ rms_norm_1` ŌĆö they never live in one tensor), our inputs are DRAM_INTERLEAVED, and our decode batch is 32 ŌĆö adding the L1 BLOCK_SHARDED bridge + a 32-row grid commitment is a structural rewrite, not a single-op swap.

`ttnn.experimental.nlp_create_qkv_heads_decode` is even stricter: it takes a **single** input with QKV packed on the last dim and emits 3 head tensors. Our Q comes from one matmul (`matmul_2`) and K from a separate matmul (`matmul_6`) on a *different* input (the rms-normed Q-latent vs the rms-normed KV-latent) ŌĆö they are not packed and cannot be concatenated without re-emitting the upstream chain.

The original queue note ("`ttnn.experimental.create_qkv_heads_from_separate_tensors`: maybe ŌĆö needs Q+K+V interleaved on hidden dim. DeepSeek MLA splits Q vs kv_a separately so this likely only fits the wq_b path; verify shapes first") turned out to be the right caveat. After inspecting `matmul_0` (the indexer/kv-latent/q-latent fused projection at `main.py:3662`), `matmul_2` (Q-up at `main.py:4191`), `matmul_5/6` (the 16-head Q-up + K-from-latent at `main.py:4594, 4644`), and the V chain (`slice_68 ŌåÆ all_gather_6 ŌåÆ sum_5 ŌåÆ slice_87 ŌåÆ rms_norm_1` at `main.py:4685-4742`), **none** of these are a QKV-pack pattern.

### Patterns / wiring lessons applicable to multiple decode-tier attempts

1. **L1-sharded outputs require a `sharded_to_interleaved` bridge before the next DRAM-interleaved op** (E5 wiring). When fusing into a kernel that outputs L1 sharded TILE (e.g. `deepseek_moe_post_combine_tilize`), budget for one extra `ttnn.sharded_to_interleaved(..., DRAM, INTERLEAVED)` step or rewrite the downstream op to accept L1 sharded input.
2. **The `nd_shard_spec` shape and grid must satisfy `num_shards_wide * num_shards_high == num_cores`** (one shard per core), and `shard_height == TILE_HEIGHT`, and `shard_width` is a multiple of 32 dividing the tensor's last dim. Compute it from the upstream op's actual padded shape.
3. **CCL fused ops (`fused_rms_minimal`, `selective_reduce_combine`, `moe_compute`, ŌĆ”) take a `GlobalSemaphore`** that must outlive the op call. Single-shot codegen needs a persistent allocation pattern ŌĆö see how the existing CCL ops (`all_to_all_combine`, `all_gather`, `reduce_scatter`) get away without it (they use `subdevice_id=None` and an internal semaphore allocator). For the fused variants we'll have to add a `ttnn.create_global_semaphore(...)` step in const_eval and pass it through.
4. **`rms_norm_post_all_gather`-style ops need BF16 input + BF16 stats AND a scaler-tile pattern in the stats** (E2 hypothesis 1). Before retrying any post-allgather fusion, dump `to_torch(stats)` at one site and confirm the per-row reduction over the gathered stats tile yields the right scalar.
5. **Blackhole vs Wormhole**: many fused kernels above were authored on Wormhole TG. Most accept a `compute_kernel_config` ŌĆö try `BlackholeComputeKernelConfig` if `WormholeComputeKernelConfig` is rejected. A genuine WH-only kernel will fail at program-factory dispatch and we should skip it. Test `@pytest.mark.requires_device(["TG"])` markers in the upstream test files for a quick sanity check.

### Stop condition

The decode device-time floor is now dominated by **structural overhead that's not a fusion target**:
- Tilize/Untilize/UntilizeWithUnpadding/TilizeWithValPadding = **19,338 ╬╝s** across 364 ops ŌĆö generic layout conversion between FP32-mid-chain and BF16-edge dtypes. The lever here is reducing FP32 intermediate tensors, not fusing more ops.
- AllGather + ReduceScatter = **5,507 ╬╝s** across 44 ops ŌĆö comm pattern dictated by the loader's shard spec. Would require resharding to reduce.
- ReshapeView = **3,090 ╬╝s** across 78 ops ŌĆö should be ~free in theory; tt-metal's ReshapeView still pays a metadata round-trip on Blackhole.
- SparseMatmul = **9,592 ╬╝s** ŌĆö already optimised to ~45 GB/s / ~37 TFLOPs per core by E31ŌĆōE34 + the E38 sparsity cleanup. Per-kernel ceiling.

No remaining single fusion-op swap has expected ROI > 1k ╬╝s on this graph without a structural rework (paged-cache reformat for flash_mla, RPŌåÆCP flip for matmul_wo, or comm-pattern restructure for async CCL). Stop the per-experiment loop here. The branch lands E38 as its terminal commit; bigger work (`flash_multi_latent_attention_decode` integration, MLA wo CP flip) is scoped as a separate project, not a single ledger row.

### Why stop here

1. **The MoE block (15,519 ╬╝s / 78% of full-model decode) is structurally bottlenecked** by the `sparse_matmul ├Ś2 + all_to_all_dispatch/combine + reduce_scatter + all_gather` pattern. Each of those individual ops is at or near its kernel ceiling. Cutting the MoE block by another 10-20% requires either re-emitting the codegen with a different sharding (matmul_wo CP flip, paged-cache reformat) or substituting a fused MoE op (`moe_compute` family ŌĆö confirmed topology-incompatible with our 4├Ś8 mesh).

2. **The MLA attention blocks (2,687 / 2,738 ╬╝s each) are at floor.** E41 SDPA + E42/E43 DCE already collapsed 30 ops per layer into ~6 ops. Further structural improvement (e.g. paged-cache packed `kvpe`, async CCL overlap) is a separate project.

3. **TM ops are bandwidth-bound, not fusion-bound.** Five separate experiments (E5/E19/E20/E21/E22/E29/E44) tried reordering or removing Tilize/Untilize/to_layout flips; only E5/E19 produced clean wins. The pattern is consistent: a TM op's cost depends on the *shape* of the data being converted (E44's lesson), so swapping op order doesn't help unless the new producer also outputs the cheaper shape.

4. **Compute density is already high** on every major op (matmuls at 4-22 TFLOPs, sparse_matmul at 22+ TFLOPs/peak hint, SDPA fused). No idle math units to recapture.

### Branch terminal commit

`ff3b10708` (E45 keep). Cumulative per-step decode: **24,038 ╬╝s** vs theoretical floor estimate of ~20,000-21,000 ╬╝s (the floor is set by aggregate `SparseMatmul + AllGather + ReduceScatter + AllToAllCombine` device time which together total ~14,000 ╬╝s ŌĆö they can't compress further without async overlap). The remaining ~3,000 ╬╝s gap to floor is mostly small TM/typecast/reshape ops that individually cost <50 ╬╝s and aren't fusion targets.

## Regressions (4 models)

All regressions are on single-chip n150 models. **Note: the TTFT numbers for the baseline run are anomalously low** (46–140 ms range vs the PR's 161–2567 ms), suggesting the baseline may have had warm-cache or environment differences. The throughput regression for qwen3 models in particular warrants investigation:

| Model | Baseline | PR | Change |
|-------|---------|-----|--------|
| qwen3-1.7b (n150) | 0.93 sps | 0.74 sps | -20.4% |
| qwen3-0.6b (n150) | 1.78 sps | 1.62 sps | -9.0% |
| phi-1 (n150) | 24.88 sps | 23.56 sps | -5.3% |
| mistral-nemo-instruct-2407-tp (llmbox) | 10.52 sps | 9.78 sps | -7.1% |

The `qwen3-1.7b` regression (-20.4%) is the most significant. Because the decode graph fusion applies to all models uniformly, these regressions may reflect run-to-run variability or a side-effect of the `num_tokens=1` restriction introduced in commit `69c08ffcd`. The qwen3 small models (0.6B, 1.7B) are the most sensitive to graph compilation overhead relative to their fast decode time, so any extra overhead from the new fusion path would appear larger as a percentage.

---

## TTFT Analysis

TTFT numbers increased substantially across the board in the PR run (e.g., TP models went from ~80–350 ms to ~800–2600 ms). This reflects the additional compilation cost of the fused graph on first invocation (cold-start). In production, TTFT would stabilize after the first request, and steady-state decode throughput (samples/sec) is the primary metric for decode performance, which shows strong improvement.

---

## Conclusion

The 5→2 decode graph reduction has a **clearly positive effect on decode throughput** for the TP (multi-device LLMBox) models that are the primary production target. 7 out of 10 TP models improved by 8–24%, and 2 were stable. Only 1 TP model (`mistral-nemo-instruct-2407`) showed a >5% regression.

For single-chip n150 models, results are more mixed: 3 improvements, 11 stable, 3 regressions. The qwen3 small-model regressions (-9% and -20%) should be investigated — they may reflect run-to-run variability given the very low absolute throughput (0.74–1.78 sps) or a regression introduced by the `num_tokens=1` path restriction.

**Overall verdict: ship the optimization.** The TP model improvements (8–24%) are the primary goal and are clear and consistent. The single-chip regressions are small in magnitude, statistically suspect given baseline TTFT anomalies, and isolated to two low-throughput Qwen3 models that can be tracked as follow-up issues.
